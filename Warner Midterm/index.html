Extreme weather, climate, and tectonic events threaten millions worldwide.

The goal of these maps is to provide information about how natural hazards play out across various geographic scales.

Earthquakes cause damage and loss of human life.

Earthquakes can technically occur anywhere, but especially occur around the circum-Pacific belt, the Alpide belt, and the mid-Atlantic belt. For more information, visit the USGS.

Weather affects us daily, while climate affects us our entire lives.

Weather and climate play important roles in human health and safety. This is especially true in the southern US, where the interaction of air masses and water conditions create unique weather and climate patterns.
